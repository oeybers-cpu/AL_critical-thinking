Strategic Approaches to Cultivate Critical Thinking

1Developing Advanced Reasoning Capabilities

Academic literacy programmes should prioritise cultivating sophisticated reasoning, complex argumentation, conceptual analysis, and innovative problem-solving abilities. These competencies transform students from passive knowledge recipients to active interrogators of disciplinary assumptions and methodologies.

2Integrating Indigenous Languages and Epistemologies

An African-centered, inclusive approach that systematically integrates local knowledge systems encourages critical reflection on cultural contexts, making learning more relevant and empowering learners to deconstruct and challenge dominant academic discourses. This approach draws on the lived experiences, historical narratives, and linguistic diversity of local communities, ensuring that academic literacy is rooted in the realities of African societies. By engaging with various dialects and indigenous languages, learners develop deeper cognitive flexibility and cultural awareness. Moreover, aligning literacy practices with local economic and social needs fosters intellectual agency and equips students to address pressing challenges within their communities.

3Implementing Holistic, Context-Specific Models

Rather than positioning Afrocentric literacy as a departure from Global North models, we advocate for a re-centering of African epistemologies within the curriculum. This approach foregrounds culturally embedded strategies that promote higher-order thinking—such as critical evaluation and problem identification—rooted in African realities. At the same time, it maintains a global orientation to knowledge production, enabling learners to engage dialogically across diverse intellectual traditions.

Transformative Pedagogical Strategies

AExplicit Teaching of Critical Dispositions

Strategic implementation of argumentation, inquiry-based learning, and dialogic approaches enables students to develop the questioning behaviours and intellectual scepticism essential for rigorous academic engagement.

BCultivating Collaborative, Dialogic Learning Environments

Fostering dialogue, structured debate, and substantive peer interaction promotes critical reflection, particularly when learners receive scaffolding to articulate, defend, and refine their intellectual positions.

CImplementing Critical Pedagogies Inspired by Freire

Dialogic classrooms equipped with tools for critical reflection help learners recognise and analyse societal contradictions, developing the critical consciousness necessary for transformative intellectual development.

Addressing Structural Challenges in Higher Education

ICurriculum Responsiveness and Assessment Reform

To genuinely develop critical thinking, curricula must demonstrate sensitivity to African socio-cultural realities, and assessments must explicitly evaluate higher-order thinking skills rather than mere content reproduction.

IIEmbedding Literacy Development Within Disciplinary Contexts

Discipline-specific approaches that integrate critical literacy principles ensure learners can navigate and challenge the epistemological foundations of their fields, promoting authentic analytical engagement.

Our Call to Action

African academic literacy development must prioritise critical thinking by embedding indigenous epistemologies, employing dialogic pedagogies, and fundamentally reforming curricula and assessments to foster higher-order reasoning and socio-cultural awareness. We urge higher education leaders, policymakers, and practitioners to move beyond technical, decontextualised literacy models towards transformative approaches that empower African intellectuals to reshape knowledge landscapes.
